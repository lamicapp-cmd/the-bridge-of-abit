The 10 Rules

How to write desires
that actually work

01
Start with "I WANT" — expressing will is more powerful than "I wish" or "I'd like".
02
Never use negation — always write in affirmative form, no "not", "never", or "without".
03
Keep it brief — maximum 14 words including "I want" and punctuation.
04
No specific people — avoid desires involving the feelings or actions of named individuals.
05
No comparisons — write without measuring yourself against others.
06
Avoid repetition — each desire must be unique, not a variant of another.
07
Don't ask for money directly — ask for what you want to do or get with it.
08
Only for you — don't include things that are mainly about other people.
09
Precise terms — write "house" not "little house", "car" not "little car".
10
Dream big — don't limit yourself to small things. Include grand, detailed ambitions.

The system

The 101 Desires
Ritual

A 5-step method to write, select, and activate your desires — and build the bridge to your ideal life.

1

Prepare two notebooks

One for drafting, one for the final list. The ritual of handwriting activates the mind differently than typing.

2

Write 150 desires in draft

Following all 10 rules. Write without filtering — selection comes later.

3

Select your best 101

Copy them carefully into your clean notebook. This list is the architect's blueprint before the building begins.

4

Read every day for a year

About 5 minutes daily. Daily reading isn't a chore — it's the fuel that keeps the engine running.

5

Celebrate and replace

When a desire comes true, mark it and replace it with a reserve one. Every fulfillment proves the bridge works.
